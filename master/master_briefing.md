## One-Paragraph Context Reset

Michigrid is a proposed cooperative infrastructure platform for Michigan communities — pre-formation, no entity filed. Organized under Michigan Cooperative Act MCL 450.3001. Dual-layer structure: cooperative layer (non-security, governance and patronage-based distributions) and project LLC layer (securities-compliant capital formation). Statutory framework: Proposition M ballot initiative. Section 7A statutory firewall separates cooperative membership from project investment. Wholesale-only MISO market access — no retail competition, no general fund exposure. Muskegon pilot: ~30MW solar (industrial/brownfield sites), ~40MW wind (Moorland, Ravenna, Casnovia townships), 20MW/80MWh battery storage hub. Website: michigrid.org. Contact: info@michigrid.org. Status: full document stack complete, attorney outreach and USPTO trademark filing are the immediate next steps.

## Legal Architecture

**Layer 1 — Cooperative Layer (Non-Security Zone)**
- Entities: Michigrid Cooperative Infrastructure Authority (MCIA) + Community Energy Cooperatives
- Governing law: Michigan Cooperative Act, MCL 450.3001 et seq.
- Character: Membership-based, not investment-based
- Benefits: Patronage-based distributions tied to use and participation — not returns on capital
- Governance: One member, one vote
- Protection: Article VIII Infrastructure Trust — prevents privatization or mission drift

**Layer 2 — Project Layer (Securities Zone — Compliant)**
- Entities: Site-specific project LLCs (e.g., Muskegon Solar I LLC)
- Capital: Institutional investors, accredited partners — Reg D, Rule 147, or applicable exemptions
- Ownership transition: Community cooperative holds milestone-triggered purchase options
- Lifecycle: 0–10 years investor priority → 10–20 years community buy-in → 20+ years community control

**The Firewall**
- Section 7A of Proposition M creates a statutory firewall between the two layers
- Cooperative membership does not confer project ownership or investment returns
- Relationship between layers is contractual only: PPAs, leases, service agreements

## Financial Flow (Simple Version)

```
MISO wholesale revenue → Project LLC
  → Debt service
  → Investor returns (years 0–10, per LLC agreement)
  → Cooperative service payments (via PPA/lease/service contracts)
      → Cooperative operations
      → Patronage distributions (usage-based, not investment returns)
      → Community Ownership Acquisition Fund (funds milestone buyouts)
      → Community programs (workforce, reinvestment)
```

---

## Five Non-Negotiable Principles

1. The cooperative layer must not be structured, described, or marketed as an investment vehicle
2. All capital formation occurs exclusively within project-specific entities — separate from cooperative membership
3. The structural firewall must be maintained in all documents, financial flows, and communications
4. Community benefit distributions are patronage-based allocations from cooperative operations — not returns on capital
5. Ownership transition occurs through contractual milestone mechanisms — not equity rights at the cooperative level
